Backgound & Motivation

A medication error involves mistakes that are caused by wrong administration or handling of drug due to ambiguity of drug label or carton. Every year, serious preventable medication errors occur in 3.8 million inpatient admissions and 3.3 million outpatient visits with an estimated annual cost burden of $20 billion. FDA collects and analyzes medication error reports to take appropriate regulatory actions to prevent these errors from happening in the future to avoid further damage. However, analysis of these semi-structured reports is a time-consuming and tedious process. Medication Error Visualization (MEV) tool is designed to help FDA analysts in the screening of critical medication error reports.
